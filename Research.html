Research Motivation

I am forever fascinated by the challenge of representing natural processes through mathematical frameworks that capture their inherent complexity. Developing appropriate statistical tools for these representations through the lens of Object Oriented Data Analysis is a core motivation that drives my work.

I am eager to contribute to projects where building and analyzing complex mathematical objects can enhance our understanding of pressing biological questions, especially in ecology, disease tracking and disease prevention. My goal is to bridge meaningful biological insight with methodological innovation, enabling nuanced and data-driven perspectives on how living systems function and evolve.

Current Research

My current postdoctoral position with Dr. Amy Willis aims to build new statistical tools for the analysis of Phylogenetic Trees. In the pursuit of this:

  • We introduced a continuous metric between phylogenetic trees with overlapping but non-identical leaves, reflecting the reality of evolutionary histories for transferred genes shared by some but not all bacteria and archea and providing an intuitive comparison method for these trees.
    • Working on:
    • The development of quick and efficient algorithms to compute the distance we introduced, along with an R package for its implementation. This involves improving the computational representation of this trees for memory efficiency.
    • Analyzing the properties of the Sample Fréchet Mean for trees in our metric space.
  • We are currently collaborating with Dr. Armeen Taeb for the devolopment of a new tool for tree reconstruction, which employs a Poset structure to guarantee stability and a low False Discovery Rate.
    • Working on:
    • Finalizing the manuscript.
    • Publishing an R package.
